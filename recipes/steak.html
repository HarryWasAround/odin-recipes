Pan Seared Ribeye Steak


Description

Neither of us are great grillers, so this recipe comes in handy. So whether you’re like us and can’t grill, or if you’re a grill master but it’s 30° outside, this recipe for Pan Seare Ribeye with Garlic, Butter and Herbs will be your new best friend.

You can serve this with just about anything as a side—baked potatoes, mashed potatoes, cheesy potatoes , a side salad, sautéed veggies, green beans, asparagus, the list goes on. Shoot, for all I care, the steak is the star of the show and doesn’t even need a side!



Ingredients

  • 1 pound 1-inch thick ribeye steak
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 4 Tablespoons oil
  • 3 Tablespoons butter
  • 2 sprigs thyme
  • 2 bunches rosemary
  • 2-4 cloves garlic, crushed (depending on the clove size)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 250°.
  2. Season the steaks evenly with salt and pepper on both sides
  3. Place the steaks on a wire rack on top of a baking sheet or 9x13 pan (whatever the wire rack fits in).
  4. Bake for 30 minutes.
  5. Heat the oil in a heavy skillet over high heat until smoking slightly.
  6. Place the steaks in the skillet and sear on one side for 30-60 seconds.
  7. Flip them and immediately add the butter, thyme, rosemary and garlic.
  8. As it is searing on the second side, tilt the skillet toward you to pool the butter.
  9. Using a spoon, continuously scoop and pour the butter over the top of the steaks, for 30-60 seconds.
  10. Rest the steak for at least 5 minutes before cutting into.
